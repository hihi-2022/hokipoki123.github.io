Being stuck on a simple problem

On the Manipulating JS objects I got a bit stuck with some assignments. I went over the error codes to find the places issues were happening and to change issues, I had been trying to reassign some values instead of just pushing them. I felt confused but not hopelessly lost like some other parts. I learnt how to better understand errors and what an error looks like if you miss an important bracket.

Elegantly solving a problem

The first part of the fizzbuzz challenge was quite an easy solve for me. You had to return a word instead of a number in some cases. So I made and if else function. When I wasn't sure about some parts I looked up other peoples solutions to see I had made a mistake in some syntax. The process felt good in the sense I felt like I knew what I was doing. I learned how to write and if else loop that returns words at certain points.

Confidence in problem solving

  1. I am not confident in my ability to pseudocode as it feels more like a stream of conscience about something I don't really understand well.
  2. I am not afraid to try something but I don't expect to get much right initially.
  3. I'm not condident with the rubberducky method as even when I explain an issue to someone I will still not understand whats going wrong only where roughly it is going wrong.
  4. Reading error messages is ok, but sometimes I can't see the exact error.
  5. Console logging I feel confident about as it allows me to read any bit im stuck to troubleshoot.
  6. Googling is ok, its not super fun trawling for you solution but it's nessesary.
  7. Asking peers for help is ok, however I'm not the best at explaining an issue.
  8. I suppose asking coaches for help isn't the biggest issue, however as I'm not the fastest I ussually look at their helping of others first.
  9. Whilst I struggle a bit I do try to mull over solutions in my head to understand them especially when I use someones elses solution as a guide.
