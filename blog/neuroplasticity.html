Neuroplasticity

Neuroplasticity relates to the brains ability to reconstruct and modify itself according to the events that happen with it. These events may be an injury or could instead be the process of learning a language. Through these events the brain can reinforce connections that allow for this learning or healing to be done.

Understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people because people can realise the influence they have over the learning process. This can allow someone to feel inspired and also increase their learning abilities aswell as their happiness.

I try to engage with principles of neuroplasticity as often as I can, I always try to consider something I am trying to learn as an achievable process that requires dedication. This already helped me through university as I believed in my ability to achieve a goal. This was different to highschool where I struggled with a fixed mindset.

Some ways to increase your neuroplasticity can be to have different experiences and consider different perspectives. Anything that challenges assumptions can help.

Research Growth Mindset

A growth mindset is a mindset in which someone is focused on improving themselves. Within a growth mindset someone believes that they have the ability to learn and improve themselves. They are not affraid of failure, rather they see it as something to learn from.

The exploration was not something entirely new to me, I have known for a little while the benefits of self belief and improvement. What it did make me think about was my reaction to failure. Sometimes if I stuggle for too long at something I can get upset and i should really just be kind to myself and have a break instead.

I will encorporate the growth mindset into my journey by simply being mindful that Dev academy is a challenge I undertook to improve myself and with that I must expect hurdles and some bad days. Ultimately, I will focus on improving myself at every point.
