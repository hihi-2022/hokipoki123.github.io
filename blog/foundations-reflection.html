Reflection

Three things I have learnt about myself and my ego are firstly that I do not like not knowing things, I always want to try to understand things I learn about. I also have found out that I can feel guilty when I’m following other peoples examples and instructions, as if I should just understand better already. I have also learned how important it can be for me to manage my expectations and to take breaks when I’m frustrated.

The role of values empathy and self awareness has been important for understanding that neither me nor other people are perfect at this stuff and therefore we should all be given slack, even if it is hard to give yourself a break.

What surprised me most about the core learning is how relevant it really is to the learning experience whether managing emotions or believing in your ability to learn.

The most challenging aspects of the core learning was putting it into practice. Even though I should meditate I often can’t be bothered or when judging my own work I have often been harsh on myself.

I think that dev academy focuses a lot on core learning because of how critical healthy self management and the ability to relate to others is in both life and the work place. So many places may leave out these aspects of learning but the benefit they provide are highly valuable. Also in learning the things that have been taught in the core learning we are more likely to succeed in the future.

No the time spent on the core learning does not feel wasted. When given the knowledge shared in the core segment you are more able to reflect on yourself and you learning which helps your learning. Also, for me at least it has felt necessary that this focus on self reflection and recognising ones own ability needs to be constantly reminded to me whenever I have felt negatively about my work.
